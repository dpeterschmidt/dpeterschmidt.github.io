Pilot

This was a side project podcast started by one of This American Life’s producers, Stephanie Foo. The idea behind this podcast (which unfortunately only has two episodes at the moment) was to introduce and give away free ideas for podcasts that anyone could use. The first episode is the only good one. Pretty simple concept, Foo and Glass both play some entertaining tape from the first radio pieces they did.

The Intern

This podcast only lasted a season, but damn if it didn’t have some of the best tape I’ve heard in audio. This podcast served as a marketing effort for the startup incubator called Betaworks. They hired this woman who hadn’t worked in tech or audio before to make a podcast about life as an intern in New York, what it’s like working for a tech company, what life is like in NY, etc. And despite this person having no experience (check) in audio, she made a pretty dang good, serialized, first person podcast. This episode comes in halfway through the series as she’s trying to figure out how to ask for a raise at her job. What follows is some of the most compelling and hard to listen tape, featuring an uncomfortable but fascinating fly on the wall perspective of her asking her boss for a raise.

Longform

Coppins is the reporter at Buzzfeed who covers Trump. He’s been covering him since 2014, since he wrote a long form piece that proclaimed that Trump would never run for president. Many think that this was the straw that broke the camel’s back and finally pushed Trump to run. Normally, I hate consuming any media that involves Trump, but Coppins tells a riveting and strange story of a weekend in 2014 that stranded him at Trump’s resort in Mara Lago. They also go into Coppins upbringing as a Mormon.

Love + Radio

I was hesitant about including this one. The producer of this episode gets cat-called in the street and instead of ignoring the guy or telling him off, she pulls out her microphone and interviews him, asking him why he cat calls. It provides a humanizing insight into these seemingly inhuman people. That doesn’t make what they’re doing okay, this just isn’t a story you hear that often.
